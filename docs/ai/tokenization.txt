================================================================================
TOKENIZATION STRATEGIES IN NLP
================================================================================

1. WHAT IS TOKENIZATION?
------------------------
The process of breaking down a raw string of text into smaller units called 
"tokens." These tokens are then mapped to numerical IDs that the model 
can understand.

2. MAJOR METHODS
----------------
* Word-level: Splits by whitespace. Problem: Huge vocabulary and "Out of 
  Vocabulary" (OOV) issues.
* Character-level: Splits into individual letters. Problem: Very long 
  sequences and loss of semantic meaning.
* Subword-level: The modern standard. Breaks rare words into meaningful 
  pieces (e.g., "unhappiness" -> "un", "happi", "ness").

3. COMMON ALGORITHMS
--------------------
* BPE (Byte Pair Encoding): Iteratively merges the most frequent pair of 
  characters/sequences. Used by GPT models.
* WordPiece: Similar to BPE but uses a likelihood-based merge. Used by BERT.
* SentencePiece: Treats whitespace as a character, making it language-agnostic.

4. SPECIAL TOKENS
-----------------
- [CLS]: Classification token (start of sequence).
- [SEP]: Separator token (ends a sentence).
- [PAD]: Padding to ensure uniform sequence length.
- [MASK]: Used in masked language modeling.
